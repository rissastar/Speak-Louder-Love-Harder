What is Cirrhosis?

Cirrhosis is a late stage of scarring (fibrosis) of the liver caused by many forms of liver diseases and conditions, such as hepatitis and chronic alcoholism.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Easy bruising and bleeding
  • Swelling in legs and abdomen
  • Jaundice
  • Confusion or drowsiness

Treatment

Treatment focuses on slowing the progression of liver damage and preventing or treating symptoms and complications. In advanced cases, a liver transplant may be necessary.

Facts and Myths

  • Myth: Cirrhosis only occurs in alcoholics.
  • Fact: Cirrhosis can result from various causes, including hepatitis and nonalcoholic fatty liver disease.
  • Myth: Cirrhosis is always fatal.
  • Fact: With proper management and treatment, many people live long, productive lives.
