What is Physical Abuse?

Physical abuse is any intentional act causing injury or trauma to another person by way of bodily contact. It includes hitting, punching, slapping, choking, pushing, or other forms of violent physical force. It's often used to exert power or control, and it can happen in any type of relationship.

Signs of Physical Abuse

  • Bruises, cuts, burns, or broken bones that are unexplained or have inconsistent stories
  • Fearfulness or anxiety around certain people
  • Wearing long sleeves or sunglasses to hide marks
  • Flinching or acting nervous when touched
  • Withdrawing from loved ones or social activities
