What is Cystic Fibrosis?

Cystic fibrosis (CF) is a progressive, genetic disease that causes persistent lung infections and limits the ability to breathe over time. It primarily affects the lungs and digestive system due to a defect in the CFTR gene, which regulates the movement of salt and water in and out of cells. Learn more.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Persistent coughing, sometimes with phlegm
  • Frequent lung infections
  • Wheezing or shortness of breath
  • Poor growth or weight gain despite good appetite
  • Salty-tasting skin
  • Difficulty with bowel movements or frequent greasy stools

Treatment Options

  • Chest physiotherapy and airway clearance
  • Inhaled medications to thin mucus and fight infection
  • Pancreatic enzyme supplements and nutritional support
  • CFTR modulator therapies like Trikafta
  • Lung transplants in advanced cases

Facts & Myths

  • Myth: CF only affects the lungs.
    Fact: It affects multiple organs, including pancreas, liver, and intestines.
  • Myth: People with CF can’t live into adulthood.
    Fact: Many live into their 40s and beyond with treatment advancements.
  • Myth: CF is contagious.
    Fact: It’s a genetic disorder, not an infection.

My Story with Cystic Fibrosis

It started with sickness. But it became survival.

I was diagnosed with Cystic Fibrosis at 7 years old, but in a way, I always knew something was wrong...

Things finally shifted in December 2021, when I started Trikafta. It was like someone finally turned the lights back on. I could breathe better, I wasn’t getting sick as often, and I had energy again.

Cystic Fibrosis stole a lot from me. My childhood. My sense of safety.
