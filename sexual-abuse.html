What Is Sexual Assault?

Sexual assault is any sexual contact or behavior that occurs without explicit consent. It can include unwanted touching, fondling, coercion, rape, or attempted rape. It's about power, not passion, and it‚Äôs never the survivor‚Äôs fault.

Signs & Myths

  • Myth: If you didn't say "no" out loud, it wasn't assault.
    Truth: Consent must be clear, enthusiastic, and ongoing. Silence does not equal consent.
  • Myth: It only counts if it was violent.
    Truth: Many survivors freeze, dissociate, or comply out of fear. Lack of resistance does not mean permission.
  • Myth: Only strangers assault people.
    Truth: Most survivors know the person who hurt them.
  • Myth: If you're not physically hurt, it's not serious.
    Truth: The emotional trauma can be lifelong, even if there are no visible injuries.
