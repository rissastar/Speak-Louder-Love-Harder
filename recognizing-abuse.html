What is Abuse?

Abuse is any behavior used to control, manipulate, or harm another person. It can take many forms—some visible, some hidden—but all are harmful.

Types of Abuse

  • Physical Abuse: Hitting, slapping, pushing, restraining, or any physical harm.
  • Emotional Abuse: Insults, threats, gaslighting, or humiliation.
  • Sexual Abuse: Any unwanted sexual contact, coercion, or violation.
  • Financial Abuse: Controlling finances, stealing, or restricting access to money.
  • Digital Abuse: Harassment or control through social media or tech.

Red Flags

  • Feeling constantly afraid of upsetting someone
  • Walking on eggshells or doubting your memory of events
  • Loss of independence or being isolated from friends/family
“Abuse thrives in silence. Your voice matters.”
