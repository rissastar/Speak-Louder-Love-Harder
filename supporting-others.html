How to Be There for Someone Grieving

Supporting someone through grief requires compassion, patience, and presence. You don't need to have all the answers — simply being there matters.

Helpful Things You Can Do

  • Listen without judgment or trying to fix it
  • Offer specific help (e.g., meals, rides, errands)
  • Respect their grieving timeline
  • Let them talk about the person they lost
  • Check in regularly — even months later

What to Avoid

  • Minimizing their loss or comparing it to others
  • Saying “they’re in a better place” unless you're sure it's comforting
  • Pushing someone to "move on"
“Grief shared is grief diminished.” – Doug Manning
