Anxiety Disorders

Overview & Understanding

Anxiety disorders are a group of mental health conditions characterized by excessive and persistent feelings of worry, fear, or nervousness. These feelings can interfere with daily activities and may manifest as physical symptoms like increased heart rate, sweating, or difficulty concentrating.

Common Types of Anxiety Disorders

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD): Persistent and excessive worry about various aspects of life.
  • Panic Disorder: Sudden and repeated panic attacks causing intense fear.
  • Social Anxiety Disorder: Extreme fear of social situations or being judged.
  • Specific Phobias: Intense fear of specific objects or situations.

Helpful Coping Strategies

  • Practice deep breathing and mindfulness meditation regularly.
  • Engage in regular physical activity to reduce stress.
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol intake.
  • Talk to trusted friends, family, or a mental health professional.
  • Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.

When to Seek Help

If anxiety feelings become overwhelming, interfere with your daily life, or lead to panic attacks, it is important to seek professional support. Therapies such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and medications can be very effective.
