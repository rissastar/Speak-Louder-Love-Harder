What Is Grief?

Grief is a natural emotional response to loss. It can stem from the death of a loved one, the end of a relationship, loss of health, or other life changes. Grief is complex, deeply personal, and never looks the same for any two people.

Common Reactions

  • Sadness, anger, confusion, guilt, numbness
  • Changes in sleep, appetite, or concentration
  • Waves of emotion that come and go unpredictably

Stages of Grief

Though everyone experiences grief differently, some go through stages such as:

  • Denial
  • Anger
  • Bargaining
  • Depression
  • Acceptance

These stages are not linear, and you may revisit them over time.

“There is no right way to grieve. There is only your way.”
